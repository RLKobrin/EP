My current research poster for my work in the ImmunoEngineering lab

My research in the ImmunoEngineering Lab at NC State focuses on the development of a hydrogel as a delivery mechanism for immunotherapeutics in the context of intratumoral injections. I have been working here since May of 2021.

A brief research summary created for BME 295 Research in Biomedical Engineering (Grade: A+)

My first day at ICN2 along with PI Arben Merkoçi and others

Over the summer of 2022, I am working a summer research internship at Institut Català de Nanociència i Nanotecnologia in the Nanobioelectronics & Biosensors Group

My research focus on the development of a contactless graphine oxide-based sensor with the ability to detect temperature, humidity, and pressure. The sensor outputs data to an app utilizing Near-Field Communication technology, giving it relevant applications for sensing in contained/sealed environments

Research Funding: Fran and George Ligler Summer 2022 Research Grant, NC State Engineer Your Experience (EYE) Grant
