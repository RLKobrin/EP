Biomechanics

BME345 Biomedical Solid Mechanics

Biomedical Solid Mechanics built upon the foundation of BME205 Intro to Biomedical Mechanics by introducing non-static systems. The class focused on both solids and dynamics, with topics including kinematics, kinetics, finite element analysis, and strain/stress transformations. Class projects included the construction of a MATLAB model of the forces and kinetics of a biker (found in the Code section).

Skills: MATLAB, Kinetics, Kinematics, FEA, Solids

BME205 Intro to Biomedical Mechanics

Intro to Biomedical Mechanics provided foundation-level knowledge of static mechanics in a biomechanics environment. I learned statics through weekly lectures and practice problems. I then had the opportunity to apply this knowledge in a practical manner through weekly hands-on labs determining the mechanics of motion of different body parts

Skills: Static Mechanics, MATLAB, Ansys FEA

Biomaterials

BME335 Biomaterials

Biomaterials built upon the foundation of BME209 through weekly literature reviews and lectures on current biomaterials trends and applications. Intended to be an introduction to the world of applied biomaterials, the course provided an overview of current research in hydrogels, tissue engineering, and more.

Skills: Research Paper Analysis

Electronics

BME207 BME Electronics

Biomedical Electronics provided the foundational knowledge for the circuitry and electronics needed for basic biomedical devices. The course consisted of lectures suplemented by a weekly hands-on lab utilizing an Arduino kit.

Skills: Arduino, Circuit Simplification, Op-Amps, Logic Gates

Biomedical Engineering Special

BME298 Biomedical Design and Manufacturing 1

Design and Manufacturing 1, known as Sophomore Design, included modules on CAD, Arduino, and Microbiology. In the CAD module, I created four CAD designs culminating in the Sophomore Design CAD Showcase. My showcase project, which won first place from a panel of industry judges, can be found in the CAD section. During the Arduino section, I worked with a breadboard and arduino kit in order to accomplish a series of projects using sensors, motors, and more. In the microbiology module, I completed an experiment testing the antibacterial properties of different nanoparticles against E.coli.

Skills: SolidWorks, Arduino, Microbiological Culture

BME295 Research in Biomedical Engineering for Undergraduates

Research in Biomedical Engineering for Undergraduates allowed me to recieve academic credit for my research conducted in the ImmunoEngineering lab under Dr. David Zaharoff. The research and final report can be found in the Research section.

Skills: Animal Cell Culture, Hydrogel Creation, Lab Safety
